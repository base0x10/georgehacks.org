    What is George Hacks Fights Human Trafficking?

    George Hacks Fights Human Trafficking is an Interdisciplinary Case Study / Innovation Marathon in which students from all backgrounds will tackle problem pitches addressing the needs presented by the NGO, Lantuun Dohio - our official partner for this event.

    Why you should care?

    Students will work on a variety of real world problems that Lantuun Dohio faces in their battle to rescue kids from human trafficking chains. The pitches (projects) range from business, engineering, management, social innovation, mental health training, storytelling skills, rebranding and many more.

    What is the structure of this event?

    George Hacks, in partnership with a Mongolian NGO, “Lantuun Dohio” is organizing a innovation marathon. Lantuun Dohio focuses on taking kids out of human trafficking circles and reintegrating them in society. The purpose of this innovation competition is to bring students from all different departments at GWU together for a 16 day period to develop solutions for the pitches presented by Lantuun Dohio. Each team will develop a proposal in collaboration with faculty and advisors selected by the George Hacks team. Office hours with each advisor will be offered throughout the two week period for students to gain insight and guidance. At the end, each team will present their solution to a panel of judges selected for their expertise across a variety of fields related to innovation and entrepreneurship.

    What will be the “theme” of this Competition?

    The theme of George Hacks Fights Human Trafficking is battling human trafficking from many different angles. Each team will incorporate the foundation of social impact, storytelling, branding, coding, engineering, or mental health expertise into a well developed and functional pitch. While giving student the opportunity to solve problems revolving around what they are passionate about, George Hacks challenges them to develop viable and well rounded solutions.

    What is an interdisciplinary hackathon/innovation competition/case study?

    At George Hacks we believe that the best solutions are created by interdisciplinary teams. All of the projects for George Hacks Fights Human Trafficking Cases Competition are made so that anyone creative and passionate about making change can hack and present a killer solution.

    Will there be prizes?

    YES. The prizes will awarded to the top three teams selected by our panel of judges after final presentations. Total value of prizes will amount to $3,000 with more details to be revealed. Keep an eye out for our social media to see the announcements.

    How do I sign up?

    Sign Up for Event

    Who can participate?

    Attendees must be an undergraduate or graduate student enrolled in a college or university, and must agree to the George Hacks Code of conduct.

    Do I need to know how to code?

    Nope! Since we are an interdisciplinary innovation organization we believe that students from all backgrounds are crucial and necessary for a viable and complete solution. At our Inaugural Medical Device Hackathon, the second place team was made up of non-engineers.

    Is this a team event?

    Yes. Either build your own team, or come to our networking sessions to meet students from a variety of fields. We’ll help you find a team that can maximize your skills. Teams should have no more than 4 people to compete.

    Will we be working the whole time?

    Sice this is a 16 day event, it will not be necessary to work non-stop. This competition will be best handled as a group project, and teams should make use of the mentors selected to advise them.     FAQs

    WHAT ARE WE?

    George Hacks is a student-led hackathon organization operating under the GW Innovation Center (Dub.Works). We are established in two universities and have over 50 members working tirelessly to grow the innovation ecosystem in DC.

    WHAT IS A HACKATHON?

    A hackathon is an innovation competition where students compete to develop the best solutions for a weakness for a chosen topic. students will have 24-hours to design, build, and present solutions to pitches presented by the judges. The top three teams will win prizes!

    WHO CAN COMPETE?

    Any student with a passion for innovation! You do not have to be an engineer or even a science major! Undergraduate students in the DC, Maryland, Virginia area can apply to participate. Submit an application here. Please submit your resume and apply through the link provided.

    WILL YOU PAY FOR MY TRAVEL TO GET THERE?

    No. We want to focus on throwing the best event possible, not moving people around. Chances are there is another hackathon near you.

    HOW MUCH DOES IT COST?

    It doesn't! George Hacks is free for all participants. (You even get free food, coffee, and gear!!)

    WHERE ARE THE EVENTS?

    At GW: The Science and Engineering Hall, 800 22nd St NW, Washington, DC 20052, B1 level by the green wall.

    At George Mason: TBD.

    At Georgetown: TBD.

    WHERE DO I SLEEP?

    Hack through the night! We'll have some gender seperated dark silent rooms but bring your own sleeping bag and pillow.

    WHAT IF I DON'T HAVE AN IDEA / TEAM / SKILLS / ETC.?

    Come anyway! There will be time to form teams if you don't have one. And we're sure everyone has something to contribute.

    WHAT PRIZES ARE THERE?

    Prizes will be announced at least two weeks before each event. In addition to their prize, each of the top three teams will receive medals.

    WHAT SHOULD I BRING?

    Bring a valid student ID, anything you need to build your project like a laptop, toiletries, a change of cloths, optionally a sleeping bag and pillow if you plan to take a nap. Surge protectors are a good idea too.

    CAN I START WORKING BEFORE THE EVENT?

    No, that's not fair. We want nice fresh projects picked straight from the field, none of those old ones that ripened on the truck.

    HOW BIG CAN MY TEAM BE?

    Up to 4 people per a team is usually most effective.

    IS THERE A CODE OF CONDUCT?

    Yes. Our event will be governed by MLH's Code of Conduct.

    HOW DOES JUDGING WORK?

    We will have two rounds of judging. The first round is a Showcase of all projects that will last an hour. The top six teams will be chosen to continue to the second round. The second round will be judged by a panel of judges made up of GWU professors and a selection of sponsors. They will choose the top three teams who will receive medals and prizes.

    WHAT IF I HAVE MORE QUESTIONS?

    Please email team@georgehacks.org with any questions you may have.

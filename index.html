  • OUR MISSION

    George Hacks is an innovation competition where students compete to develop the best solution for a given need in the medical device market. The theme of our hackathon: Assistive Devices that address the need for affordable devices among patients with disabilities.

    ARE YOU READY TO CONTRIBUTE TO THE FUTURE OF MEDICAL DEVICES?

    Do you want to be on the frontlines of the creation of the most innovative assistive devices? George Hacks: Assistive Devices Medical Hackathon is a competition that brings together a community of inventive and created students to compete to develop the best solution for the chosen weakness in the medical device market.

    FAQ

    WHAT ARE WE?

    George Hacks: Assistive Devices is the first medical hackathon hosted by the George Washington University. We bring together the most innovative students from all kinds of fields for a 24-hour competition.

    WHAT IS A MEDICAL HACKATHON?

    A medical hackathon is an innovation competition where students compete to develop the best solutions for a weakness in the medical device market for a chosen focus area. students will have 24-hours to design, build, and present solutions to pitches presented by the judges. The top three teams will win prizes!

    It doesn't! George Hacks is free for all participants.

    No. We want to focus on throwing the best event possible, not moving people around. Chances are there is another hackathon near you.
